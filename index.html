My Approach

When nobody internally truly owns your IT controls, things can get messy fast. I've seen this play out over and over. Controls pile up because an auditor once asked for them, no one understands why certain processes are in place, and before long the entire program becomes bloated and difficult to manage. Controls that should take 10 minutes end up eating your team's whole afternoon.

My goal is to help companies build control programs that actually work, without making them more complicated than they need to be. I kept seeing the same thing over and over: companies spending more time than they should on IT controls, and still not ending up with an effective program.

Having spent years both auditing IT controls and helping companies build them, I know what auditors actually need, and just as importantly, what they don't. When something comes up, I help teams fix the actual problem instead of adding more process to cover it.

Designing a good control isn't really the hard part. Getting people to actually follow it, quarter after quarter, year after year - that's the hard part. One of my favorite parts of this work is partnering with the people who actually own and operate these processes. Not just delivering recommendations, but sitting down with your team to figure out what works, and building something they can understand and maintain.

A lot of my job is helping clients separate what truly matters from what does not. I want clients spending less time worrying about IT controls and more time running their business.
