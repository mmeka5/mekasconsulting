Services

  • Ongoing IT SOX Advisory
    Dedicated weekly support for companies that need senior IT SOX guidance without hiring a full-time resource; this typically includes support such as:

    • Advising on control design, documentation, and risk assessment

    • Leading walkthroughs, audit prep, and remediations

    • Helping teams think through practical ways to meet SOX expectations

    • Being available for check-ins, time-sensitive issues, and internal planning discussions as they arise

  • IT Control Design and Implementation
    Whether you're building from scratch or refining existing controls, I bring the experience to cut through the noise and focus on what matters — designing effective, reasonable control solutions. This can also include working with control owners to support the execution of those controls.
  • SOX Readiness and Remediation Support
    I help companies prepare for SOX or remediate control gaps after an audit — designing sustainable, risk-based controls that meet requirements without unnecessary complexity. My approach is grounded in experience and tailored to what makes sense for your business.

    I help your team create control documentation that clearly defines the purpose and operation of your controls — streamlining internal understanding and audit readiness.

Client Scenarios

Companies often bring me in when they’re facing one of the following situations — and need consistent, senior-level support to get things right and keep them moving.

  • Preparing for SOX for the first time, often under tight deadlines
  • Navigating control gaps after an audit
  • Rolling out a new system without formal IT controls in place
  • Looking for a steady resource to support IT risk, access design, and control documentation

In these situations, I typically support clients at around 15 hours per week — enough to drive progress without the need for a full-time hire. That said, I’m flexible with timing and involvement depending on your needs.

“When controls are designed effectively from the beginning, everything downstream — from operations to audits and testing — becomes far more efficient and manageable.”
